Visual Organization of Grammar Concepts

Mind maps help you see the big picture and understand how different grammar elements connect. This visual approach makes complex grammar relationships clear and memorable.

English Grammar
TENSES
Present Tenses
Actions happening now or regularly
Simple Present: I work daily
Present Continuous: I am working now
Present Perfect: I have worked here for 5 years
Present Perfect Continuous: I have been working since morning
Past Tenses
Actions completed in the past
Simple Past: I worked yesterday
Past Continuous: I was working at 3pm
Past Perfect: I had worked before you came
Past Perfect Continuous: I had been working for hours
Future Tenses
Actions that will happen
Simple Future: I will work tomorrow
Future Continuous: I will be working at 5pm
Future Perfect: I will have worked 8 hours by evening
Future Perfect Continuous: I will have been working for 10 hours
MODAL VERBS
Ability
Expressing what someone can do
Can: I can swim (present ability)
Could: I could swim when I was young (past ability)
Permission
Asking for or giving permission
May: May I leave early? (formal)
Can: Can I use your phone? (informal)
Might: Might I suggest something? (very polite)
Obligation
What you must or should do
Must: You must finish this today (strong)
Have to: I have to work late (external obligation)
Should: You should exercise more (advice)
Probability
How likely something is to happen
Will: It will rain tomorrow (95% certain)
May/Might: It might be true (30-50% certain)
Could: It could happen (possible)
Advice
Recommendations and suggestions
Should: You should see a doctor
Ought to: We ought to help them (moral duty)
Had better: You'd better hurry (warning)
üí° Key Insight: Every English sentence uses either a tense or a modal verb to show time and attitude!

üîë Answer Key

1. C) have studied - Present Perfect shows an action that started in the past and continues to affect the present.
2. C) May - "May I...' is the most polite and formal way to ask for permission.
3. C) It will rain tomorrow - "Will' expresses the highest level of certainty (95%).
4. B) Past Continuous - "Was reading' shows an ongoing action in the past that was interrupted.
5. will have worked / will have been working - Future Perfect shows completion by a future time.
6. should - "Should' is used to give advice or make recommendations.
7. has been - Present Perfect Continuous emphasizes duration of ongoing action.
8. "I have known him for five years." - Use Present Perfect with stative verbs like "know".
9. "You must complete this homework." - Modal verbs are followed by base form (no "to").
10. Sample: "I will be working at the office tomorrow at 3 PM." - Future Continuous for ongoing future action.
11. Sample: "You should practice speaking English every day to improve." - "Should' for advice.
12. Sample: "I had finished my homework before my friends arrived." - Past Perfect shows earlier action.
