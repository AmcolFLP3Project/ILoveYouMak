Master the art of word building through systematic patterns. Understanding prefixes, suffixes, and root words allows you to expand your vocabulary exponentially and decode unfamiliar words in academic contexts.

Word Building Mastery
PREFIXES
UN- (not, opposite)
unchanged, unclear, unable, unusual, unfair
Academic usage: unconventional, unprecedented, unbiased, undetermined
RE- (again, back)
research, respond, require, represent
Academic usage: reevaluate, reconstruct, reinterpret, redistribute
PRE- (before)
predict, prevent, prepare, precise
Academic usage: predetermined, prerequisite, preliminary, presumption
DE- (down, away, reverse)
define, derive, design, demonstrate
Academic usage: deduction, decontextualize, deconstruct, depreciate
SUFFIXES
NOUN SUFFIXES
-tion/-sion: creation, conclusion, analysis, revision
-ment: assessment, establishment, development
-ity/-ty: identity, variety, complexity, security
-ance/-ence: significance, evidence, occurrence
ADJECTIVE SUFFIXES
-al: functional, structural, cultural, ethical
-ive: creative, objective, distinctive, alternative
-ic: economic, academic, systematic, specific
-able/-ible: available, accessible, flexible, visible
VERB SUFFIXES
-ize/-ise: analyze, categorize, emphasize, summarize
-ify: classify, identify, specify, modify
-ate: evaluate, estimate, indicate, generate
ROOT WORDS
SPECT- (see, look)
inspect, respect, expect, aspect, perspective, prospect, retrospective
STRUCT- (build)
structure, construct, instruct, destruct, infrastructure, reconstruction
DICT- (speak, say)
predict, indicate, contradict, dictate, dictionary, jurisdiction
CERT- (sure, certain)
certain, certificate, certify, uncertainty, ascertain

ðŸŽ¯ Answer Key & Explanations

1. B) analysis - Need a noun to complete "conducted a thorough ___". "Analysis" is the noun form of "analyze."
2. A) economical - "Economical" means cost-effective or thrifty. "Economic" relates to the economy as a system.
3. B) re- - "Reconstruct" means to build again or rebuild theories with new understanding.
4. D) distraction - "Distraction" comes from "tract" (pull/draw), not "struct" (build). The others all relate to building/structure.
5. C) significance - This is the standard noun form. "Signification" exists but is less commonly used in academic writing.
6. B) evaluate - Need the verb form after "will." "The committee will evaluate" is correct future tense.
7. C) -ive - The suffix "-ive" creates adjectives (creative, objective, distinctive). Others create nouns or verbs.
8. C) methodically - Adverbs typically end in "-ly." "Methodically" means "in a systematic way."
9. B) indicate - Need the verb form. "The findings indicate" shows what the research demonstrates.
10. D) un- - "Un-" is the most common prefix meaning "not" (unclear, unable, unfair).
